Background

Power Drills are a useful tool when it comes to home projects or even construction or carpentry work. For most people these devices are easy to use, as they are portable and easy to hold. For others however, using a power drill is not so easy a task
For people with hand or wrist tendonitis, the grip strength required to operate a power drill is often much greater than they can provide. We designed the DrillAssist so that people with hand or wrist tendonitis can use power drills without the extended use of their hands and wrists.

Product Pitch

Design

The DrillAssist features a sleek modular design, which makes it usable to anyone. The unit can be easily removed from, and reattached to the power drill itself. The product has two sets of ergonomic padding, and a velcro secured arm brace, allowing the user to comfortably lift and operate a power drill.

Recommended Users

This product is recommended for individuals with hand or wrist tendonitis, who have difficulties using power drills. The Drill Assist allows the user to comfortably use a Power drill without putting any strain on their hands or wrists.

Cost

The cost of the combined DrillAssist and Power Drill is $224.22 (USD), where the base price of the Power Drill itself is $160.00 (USD).

Manufacturing

Manufacturing methods for the DrillAssist include die casting, welding, tapping, and drilling. These methods were chosen to optimize performance and minimize cost.

Testing

The DrillAssist model is tested for modes of failure using both hand calculations and FEA analysis.

Function

The DrillAssist is durable and can withstand high amounts of pressure and torque. It is suitable for use in a heavy construction work environment.

Performance & Specifications

The frame DrillAssist is made primarily from Aluminum A360, with neoprene foam padding and nylon straps.
The DrillAssist is designed to withstand greater amounts of force and torque than the power drill itself, thus not limiting the drill's ability.
